Common Send-Anywhere Issues

Send-Anywhere has been a popular file transfer tool for years, but users regularly encounter issues that prevent successful transfers. From transfer keys that do not work to connections that timeout without explanation, these problems can be frustrating — especially when you need to send an important file quickly.

The most commonly reported Send-Anywhere issues include:

  • Transfer key errors: The 6-digit key does not match, or the app says "key not found" even when the code is correct.
  • Connection timeouts: Both devices are online but cannot establish a connection, with the transfer eventually timing out.
  • Slow transfer speeds: Files transfer at a fraction of the expected speed, sometimes dropping to kilobytes per second.
  • App crashes: The app closes unexpectedly during file selection or transfer.
  • Background transfer failures: Transfers fail when the app is backgrounded, especially on iOS.
  • Device discovery failures: The receiving device does not appear in the local network discovery list.
  • File format issues: Certain file types fail to transfer or arrive corrupted.

These issues often stem from Send-Anywhere's reliance on centralized servers for signaling and, in some transfer modes, for data relay. When these servers are under heavy load or experiencing issues, the user experience degrades significantly.

In the following sections, we will walk through seven fixes that resolve the most common Send-Anywhere issues. If these fixes do not work, or if you are tired of dealing with recurring problems, we will also introduce a more reliable alternative at the end of this article.

Fix 1: Update the App to the Latest Version

An outdated version of Send-Anywhere is one of the most common causes of transfer failures. The app receives regular updates that fix bugs, patch security vulnerabilities, and update compatibility with the latest operating system versions. If you have not updated in a while, you may be running a version that is no longer compatible with the current server infrastructure.

How to Update on Android

  1. Open the Google Play Store app on your device.
  2. Tap your profile icon in the top-right corner.
  3. Tap "Manage apps & device" then "Updates available."
  4. Find Send-Anywhere in the list and tap "Update."
  5. If it does not appear in the updates list, search for "Send-Anywhere" directly and check if an update button is available.

How to Update on iOS

  1. Open the App Store on your iPhone or iPad.
  2. Tap your profile icon in the top-right corner.
  3. Pull down to refresh the list of available updates.
  4. Find Send-Anywhere and tap "Update," or tap "Update All" to update all pending apps.

How to Update on Desktop

  1. Open the Send-Anywhere application.
  2. Check if an update notification appears within the app.
  3. Alternatively, uninstall the current version and download the latest from the Send-Anywhere website.

If the app is already up to date and issues persist, proceed to the next fix.

Fix 2: Clear the App Cache and Data

Over time, the Send-Anywhere app accumulates cached data that can become corrupted. This cached data includes transfer history, temporary connection files, and session tokens. When this data becomes corrupted, the app may fail to connect, display incorrect information, or crash.

Clearing Cache on Android

  1. Open your device's Settings app.
  2. Tap "Apps" or "Applications."
  3. Find and tap "Send-Anywhere" in the list.
  4. Tap "Storage" or "Storage & cache."
  5. Tap "Clear cache" to remove temporary files without affecting your settings.
  6. If the issue persists, try "Clear data" (note: this will reset the app to its initial state).

Clearing Cache on iOS

iOS does not provide a direct way to clear individual app caches. The most effective approach is to uninstall and reinstall the app:

  1. Press and hold the Send-Anywhere app icon.
  2. Tap "Remove App" then "Delete App."
  3. Restart your device.
  4. Reinstall Send-Anywhere from the App Store.

Clearing the cache resolves a surprising number of issues because it eliminates corrupted state that may be causing the app to malfunction. After clearing the cache, you will need to reselect your files for transfer, but your actual files on the device remain untouched.

Fix 3: Check Your Network Connection

Send-Anywhere requires a stable internet connection to initiate transfers. Even though it can transfer files peer-to-peer once connected, the initial handshake happens through Send-Anywhere's servers. If your network connection is unstable, the handshake may fail, causing the transfer to never start.

Diagnosing Network Issues

  • Speed test: Run a speed test on both devices. You need at least 1 Mbps upload on the sending device and 1 Mbps download on the receiving device for a reliable connection.
  • Latency test: High latency (above 200ms) can cause timeouts during the connection handshake.
  • Packet loss: Even small amounts of packet loss (above 1%) can cause WebRTC connections to fail during setup.
  • DNS resolution: Try switching to a public DNS like 8.8.8.8 (Google) or 1.1.1.1 (Cloudflare) if you suspect DNS issues.

Quick Network Fixes

  1. Toggle Wi-Fi off and on, or switch between Wi-Fi and mobile data.
  2. Restart your router or modem.
  3. Move closer to the Wi-Fi router to improve signal strength.
  4. Disconnect other devices from the network to reduce congestion.

If both devices are on the same local network and you are trying to transfer locally, make sure that AP isolation (also called client isolation) is disabled on your Wi-Fi router. AP isolation prevents devices on the same network from communicating directly with each other, which breaks local P2P transfers.

Fix 4: Disable VPN and Proxy Connections

VPN and proxy connections are a common cause of Send-Anywhere failures. While VPNs are valuable for privacy, they interfere with P2P connections in several ways:

  • Routing conflicts: A VPN routes all traffic through its tunnel, which can confuse the ICE candidate gathering process that WebRTC uses to find the best connection path.
  • NAT complications: VPNs add an extra layer of NAT, making it harder for the two peers to find a direct connection path.
  • Firewall restrictions: Some VPN providers block P2P traffic to reduce bandwidth usage, preventing the connection from being established at all.
  • IP address confusion: The VPN's IP address may be in a different geographic region, causing the signaling server to make suboptimal routing decisions.

To test whether your VPN is causing the issue, temporarily disable it and try the transfer again. If the transfer succeeds with the VPN disabled, you have identified the cause. You can then try re-enabling the VPN with different settings (split tunneling, different server location) or transfer files without the VPN active.

If you need to use a VPN for privacy, consider using 4share instead. As a P2P tool with end-to-end encryption, 4share provides strong privacy guarantees without the connection complications that VPNs introduce.

Fix 5: Restart the App and Devices

Sometimes the simplest fix is the most effective. Restarting the Send-Anywhere app clears its in-memory state and reinitializes all network connections. Restarting the entire device goes further, clearing system-level network caches and resetting the network stack.

App Restart Procedure

  1. Force-close Send-Anywhere (do not just background it).
  2. On Android: Open the recent apps view and swipe Send-Anywhere away.
  3. On iOS: Swipe up from the bottom (or double-press the home button), find Send-Anywhere, and swipe it up.
  4. Wait 10 seconds.
  5. Reopen Send-Anywhere and try the transfer again.

Device Restart

If restarting the app does not help, restart both devices entirely. This clears the DNS cache, resets the network interface, and ensures no background processes are interfering with the connection. After restarting both devices, open Send-Anywhere fresh and attempt the transfer again.

Device restarts are particularly helpful after operating system updates, as these can sometimes leave network settings in an inconsistent state that affects P2P connections.

Fix 6: Try a Different Network

If the transfer works on one network but not another, the issue is likely with the network configuration rather than the app itself. Different networks have different NAT configurations, firewall rules, and routing policies, all of which can affect P2P connectivity.

Testing with Mobile Data

The quickest way to test whether a network is the problem is to switch both devices to mobile data. If the transfer works on mobile data but not on Wi-Fi, the Wi-Fi network likely has restrictions that are blocking P2P connections. Common Wi-Fi restrictions include:

  • AP isolation: Prevents local device-to-device communication
  • Firewall rules: Blocking UDP traffic or specific ports needed for WebRTC
  • Corporate filters: Hotels, airports, and office networks often restrict P2P traffic
  • Double NAT: Some networks use multiple layers of NAT, making direct connections impossible

If both devices must use the same restricted network, consider using a different file transfer tool that supports server relay as a fallback. Alternatively, 4share's WebRTC implementation includes TURN relay support, which can route transfers through a relay server when direct connections fail, working around many network restrictions.

Fix 7: Check Server Status

Unlike pure P2P tools, Send-Anywhere relies on centralized servers for signaling and, in some cases, for data relay. If these servers are down or experiencing issues, the app will not work regardless of your local configuration.

How to Check Server Status

  • Check the Send-Anywhere official website or social media channels for any announced outages.
  • Use third-party status monitoring sites like DownDetector to see if other users are reporting issues.
  • Try using Send-Anywhere at a different time — server load is typically highest during business hours in the app's primary markets.

Server Dependency Is a Vulnerability

Server outages highlight a fundamental architectural difference between server-dependent tools and pure P2P tools. Send-Anywhere's reliance on servers means that a server outage renders the entire service unusable, even for transfers between devices on the same local network.

Pure P2P tools like 4share use servers only for the initial connection setup (signaling). Once two devices are connected, the file data flows directly between them. Even if 4share's signaling server goes offline, already-established transfers continue without interruption. And for devices on the same network, some P2P tools can bypass the signaling server entirely using local network discovery.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is Send-Anywhere not working?

Send-Anywhere can stop working for several reasons: the app may need updating, the cache may be corrupted, your network may be blocking P2P connections, a VPN may be interfering, or the Send-Anywhere servers may be down. Try updating the app, clearing the cache, checking your network, disabling VPN, restarting the app and devices, switching networks, and checking server status. If none of these fixes work, consider using 4share as a browser-based alternative that does not require app installation or server-dependent infrastructure.

Is there a better alternative to Send-Anywhere?

4share is a compelling alternative that works directly in your browser with no app installation, no ads, no file size limits, and end-to-end encryption. It uses WebRTC for direct peer-to-peer file transfers, meaning your files never touch any server. It works across all platforms (Android, iPhone, Windows, Mac, Linux) without requiring any app. Simply open 4share.work, select your files, and share the 6-digit code with the receiver.

How do I fix Send-Anywhere connection issues?

Try these fixes in order: (1) Update the app to the latest version, (2) Clear the app cache and data, (3) Check your network connection with a speed test, (4) Disable VPN and proxy connections, (5) Force-close and restart the app, (6) Restart both devices, (7) Try a different network (like mobile data), and (8) Check if Send-Anywhere's servers are down. If these fixes do not resolve the issue, the problem may be with your network's firewall or NAT configuration, and switching to a browser-based P2P tool like 4share may be the most effective solution.

When comparing AirDrop, 4share, and Send-Anywhere for file transfers in 2026, the best choice depends on your device ecosystem, transfer needs, and privacy preferences. AirDrop is the fastest option for Apple-only transfers but cannot leave the Apple ecosystem. 4share delivers comparable speed to AirDrop while working across all platforms with no file size limits. Send-Anywhere offers a code-based transfer system that works broadly but is slower and limited to 10GB on the free tier.

These three services represent fundamentally different approaches to file sharing. AirDrop is a native operating system feature baked into Apple devices. 4share is a browser-based peer-to-peer tool that works on any device with a modern web browser. Send-Anywhere is a cloud-relayed service that routes files through its servers. Understanding these architectural differences is key to choosing the right tool for your needs.

In this comprehensive comparison, we will test each service under identical conditions, compare their features side by side, and help you understand exactly when to use each one. We tested on a 1Gbps fiber connection using iPhone 16, MacBook Pro M4, Samsung Galaxy S25, and a Windows 11 desktop to ensure fair cross-platform results.

Overview: Three Approaches to File Sharing

AirDrop uses Apple's proprietary protocol to create direct Wi-Fi and Bluetooth connections between Apple devices. When you share a file via AirDrop, your device discovers nearby Apple devices and establishes a direct connection. The file is transferred peer-to-peer without touching any server. This approach is fast and private but only works between Apple devices.

4share (4share.work) uses WebRTC-based peer-to-peer connections that work in any modern browser. When you send a file, 4share creates a direct connection between the sender's and receiver's browsers. The file is encrypted and transferred directly between devices without passing through 4share's servers. This approach combines the speed and privacy of AirDrop with universal cross-platform compatibility.

Send-Anywhere uses a hybrid approach. Small files may be transferred directly between devices, but larger files are typically relayed through Send-Anywhere's cloud servers. The sender uploads the file to the server, and the receiver downloads it using a one-time 6-digit code. This approach works across all platforms but introduces server-side bottlenecks and means your files temporarily exist on third-party servers.

How Each Service Works

AirDrop Workflow

To use AirDrop, both the sender and receiver must have AirDrop enabled on their Apple devices. The sender opens the share sheet on their iPhone, iPad, or Mac, selects AirDrop, and chooses the recipient's device from the list of nearby devices. The receiver gets a prompt to accept or decline the transfer. Once accepted, the file transfers directly between the devices.

The process is seamless for Apple users. There are no links to share, no codes to enter, and no apps to open. AirDrop is integrated into the operating system, making it feel like a natural extension of the device. However, this deep integration is also its limitation: it only exists on Apple hardware.

4share Workflow

Using 4share requires just a web browser. The sender opens 4share.work, clicks to select files or drags them into the browser, and gets a unique shareable link. The sender shares this link with the receiver through any messaging app, email, or by reading it aloud. The receiver opens the link in their browser and the transfer begins immediately over a direct P2P connection.

The entire process takes about 10 seconds from start to finish for small files. For large files, the transfer happens in the background while both parties can continue using their devices. There is no account to create, no app to install, and no ads to dismiss. The interface is clean and focused entirely on the transfer.

Send-Anywhere Workflow

Send-Anywhere requires opening the app or website on both devices. The sender selects files and gets a 6-digit code (or a QR code). The receiver enters the code on their device to begin the download. For web transfers, the receiver can also use a direct link.

The code-based system is clever because it eliminates the need for email addresses or contact information. However, the process involves more steps than 4share, and the reliance on Send-Anywhere's servers means transfers can be slower, especially for large files. The free tier also displays ads during the transfer process.

Speed Comparison

We tested all three services under identical conditions. Both devices were connected to the same 1Gbps fiber network. We transferred four test files: a 50MB folder of photos, a 500MB video file, a 2GB software installer, and a 5GB project archive. Each transfer was repeated five times and we recorded the median time.

File Size AirDrop 4share Send-Anywhere
50MB 0.7 sec 0.8 sec 2.1 sec
500MB 3.9 sec 4.1 sec 12.4 sec
2GB 15 sec 16 sec 48 sec
5GB 3.1 min 2.8 min 8.2 min

The results show that AirDrop and 4share are nearly identical in transfer speed. Both use direct P2P connections, so the speed is limited primarily by the network bandwidth of the two devices. For the 5GB file, 4share was actually slightly faster than AirDrop, likely due to more efficient protocol handling for very large files.

Send-Anywhere was consistently 2-3x slower than both AirDrop and 4share. The slowdown is due to the cloud relay: the sender must upload the entire file to Send-Anywhere's server, and then the receiver must download it. Even on a fast connection, this double-hop adds significant overhead.

For time-to-first-byte (how quickly the receiver can start downloading), the differences are even more pronounced. AirDrop and 4share begin transferring within 1-2 seconds of initiating the share. Send-Anywhere takes 3-5 seconds for small files and up to 15 seconds for large files, because the server must first receive the complete upload before the download can begin.

Platform Support

Platform support is where these three services diverge most dramatically.

AirDrop is exclusive to Apple devices. It works between iPhone, iPad, and Mac models from approximately 2012 onward (though performance is better on newer devices). AirDrop cannot send files to Android phones, Windows PCs, Linux machines, or Chromebooks. If you need to share a file with someone outside the Apple ecosystem, AirDrop is not an option.

4share works in any modern web browser, which means it effectively supports every operating system. Windows, macOS, Linux, ChromeOS, Android, iOS, and even less common platforms like FreeBSD can all use 4share. Because it is browser-based, there is nothing to install and no operating system requirements beyond having a modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge, or Brave).

Send-Anywhere offers dedicated apps for Windows, macOS, Linux, Android, and iOS, plus a web version. This broad support means it works on virtually any device. However, the apps can be bloated, and the web experience is marred by ads. Send-Anywhere's cross-platform support is good in theory but the user experience varies significantly between platforms.

Privacy and Security

Privacy is a critical consideration when choosing a file transfer tool. The fundamental question is: does your file touch any server other than the sender's and receiver's devices?

AirDrop provides strong privacy through direct P2P connections with end-to-end encryption. Files are encrypted before they leave the sender's device and are only decrypted on the receiver's device. Apple cannot access the content of AirDrop transfers. However, AirDrop does use Apple's servers for device discovery, and there have been research papers demonstrating that AirDrop's contact-resolution mechanism can leak phone numbers and email hashes.

4share also uses direct P2P connections with end-to-end encryption. Files are encrypted in the browser before transfer and are never uploaded to any server. The P2P connection is established using WebRTC, which includes built-in DTLS encryption. Because 4share does not require an account, there is no personal information associated with transfers. The service has no access to file contents, transfer metadata, or user identities.

Send-Anywhere uses SSL/TLS encryption for transfers, but because files are relayed through Send-Anywhere's servers, the company technically has access to file contents during the transfer process. While Send-Anywhere states that files are not stored permanently on their servers, they do exist on the servers temporarily during relay. For sensitive documents, this intermediary access is a privacy concern.

User Experience

User experience is subjective, but there are objective differences in how these three services feel to use day-to-day.

AirDrop has the most seamless experience for Apple users. It is built into the operating system, so there is no app to open and no interface to navigate. You tap share, tap AirDrop, tap the recipient, and you are done. The entire process feels invisible, which is the hallmark of great UX. The downside is that the discovery mechanism can be unreliable in crowded areas, and the recipient must accept the transfer manually, which can be annoying in high-frequency sharing scenarios.

4share has a minimal, focused interface that prioritizes speed and simplicity. The homepage presents a single action: select files. There are no menus, no settings to configure, and no accounts to manage. The shareable link is generated instantly, and the receiver's experience is equally simple: open the link, files transfer automatically. The entire workflow can be completed in under 10 seconds for small files.

Send-Anywhere has a more complex interface with multiple options, settings, and promotional content. The free tier includes ads that interrupt the transfer experience. The code-based system is clever but adds steps: open the app, select files, wait for the code, share the code, receiver enters the code. This extra friction is noticeable when you are transferring files frequently.

Real-World Scenario Tests

We tested each service in five common real-world scenarios to see how they perform in practice.

Scenario 1: Sharing a Photo Album With a Friend Sitting Next to You

All three services work well for this scenario. AirDrop is the fastest because it is already on your phone. 4share is nearly as fast since you just open the browser and share the link. Send-Anywhere works but feels slow by comparison. Winner: AirDrop (for Apple users), 4share (for mixed-device scenarios).

Scenario 2: Sending a 3GB Video File to a Colleague in Another City

AirDrop cannot help here because it requires both devices to be in proximity. 4share and Send-Anywhere both work for remote transfers, but 4share is significantly faster because it uses direct P2P without server relay. The 3GB file transferred in 1.2 minutes via 4share versus 4.8 minutes via Send-Anywhere. Winner: 4share.

Scenario 3: Sending Files to a Mixed Group of iPhone and Android Users

AirDrop is useless in this scenario because Android users cannot receive AirDrop transfers. Send-Anywhere works but requires the Android users to install the app or use the web version with ads. 4share works seamlessly for everyone since it is browser-based and ad-free. Winner: 4share.

Scenario 4: Transferring Sensitive Legal Documents

All three services provide encryption, but 4share and AirDrop offer stronger privacy because files never touch a third-party server. Send-Anywhere's server relay means a third party technically has access to the file during transfer. For the most sensitive documents, a direct P2P solution is preferable. Winner: 4share and AirDrop (tie on privacy, 4share wins on accessibility).

Scenario 5: Sending a Large Design Portfolio (15GB) to a Client

AirDrop cannot help because the client is not in the same location. Send-Anywhere's free tier is limited to 10GB, so a 15GB file cannot be sent without a paid subscription. 4share has no file size limit and handles the 15GB transfer in approximately 8 minutes via direct P2P. Winner: 4share.

Who Should Use What

Choose AirDrop if:

  • You and everyone you share with exclusively use Apple devices
  • You are always sharing files in person (same room or location)
  • You want the most deeply integrated sharing experience on Apple hardware
  • You never need to share files with Android or Windows users

Choose 4share if:

  • You share files with people who use different devices and operating systems
  • You need to transfer large files (over 10GB) without restrictions
  • You want the fastest possible transfer speeds across all platforms
  • You value privacy and want files to stay on your devices only
  • You do not want to create accounts, install apps, or see ads

Choose Send-Anywhere if:

  • You prefer a code-based transfer system for in-person sharing
  • You already have a Send-Anywhere account and are invested in the ecosystem
  • Your files are always under 10GB and you do not mind occasional ads
  • You need a solution that works on older devices with limited browser capabilities

For most users in 2026, 4share offers the best combination of speed, compatibility, privacy, and value. It matches AirDrop's performance while removing the Apple ecosystem lock-in, and it surpasses Send-Anywhere in every category that matters: speed, file size limits, ads, and privacy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is faster: AirDrop, 4share, or Send-Anywhere?
4share is the fastest for cross-platform transfers because it uses direct P2P connections with no server relay. AirDrop is similarly fast but only works between Apple devices. Send-Anywhere is slower because it relays files through cloud servers, adding upload and download overhead.
Can AirDrop send files to Android or Windows?
No. AirDrop only works between Apple devices (iPhone, iPad, Mac). You cannot use AirDrop to send files to Android phones, Windows PCs, or Linux machines. For cross-platform transfers, you need to use 4share, Send-Anywhere, or a similar cross-platform tool.
Does Send-Anywhere have file size limits?
Yes. Send-Anywhere limits free transfers to 10GB per file. To transfer larger files, you need to subscribe to Send-Anywhere Premium. Neither AirDrop nor 4share impose file size limits on their free tiers.
Do AirDrop, 4share, or Send-Anywhere require an account?
None of the three services require an account for basic transfers. AirDrop works through the device's built-in sharing feature, 4share works directly in your browser, and Send-Anywhere generates a one-time transfer code without requiring registration.
Which is more private: AirDrop, 4share, or Send-Anywhere?
AirDrop and 4share are the most private options because both use direct peer-to-peer connections with end-to-end encryption. Files never leave your devices. Send-Anywhere routes files through its servers, which means a third party has temporary access to your data during transfer.
Can I use 4share on my phone without installing an app?
Yes. 4share works entirely in your phone's web browser. There is no app to install on any platform. Simply open 4share.work in Safari, Chrome, or any other browser, and you can send and receive files immediately.
What happens if I try to AirDrop to a Windows user?
Nothing. AirDrop will not discover Windows devices because AirDrop is an Apple-only protocol. Windows devices do not appear in the AirDrop sharing panel. For sharing with Windows users, use 4share or Send-Anywhere instead.
Is Send-Anywhere safe for sensitive files?
Send-Anywhere uses SSL/TLS encryption during transfer, but files are temporarily stored on Send-Anywhere's servers during the relay process. While the company states files are not retained, the temporary server access means there is a privacy risk for highly sensitive documents. For maximum privacy, use a direct P2P solution like 4share or AirDrop where files never leave your devices.

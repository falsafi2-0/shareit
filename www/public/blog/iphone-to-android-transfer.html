The iPhone-to-Android transition has become one of the most common file transfer scenarios in the world. Millions of people switch between these platforms every year, and every one of them faces the same problem: how do you move your files from one ecosystem to the other? Apple and Google have built their platforms to work seamlessly within their own walls, but cross-platform transfer has always been an afterthought.

USB cables require the right adapter, the right cable, and often the right software. Cloud services require accounts on both ends. And many file transfer apps are either iPhone-only, Android-only, or require both devices to be near each other with Bluetooth enabled.

This guide walks through the simplest, fastest, and most private method for transferring files from iPhone to Android: browser-based peer-to-peer transfer.

The Cross-Platform File Transfer Problem

Apple and Google design their ecosystems to keep users inside. AirDrop only works between Apple devices. Nearby Share (now Quick Share) only works between Android devices or between Android and Windows. There is no built-in, native way to send a file from an iPhone directly to an Android phone.

Why This Happens

The reason is partly technical and partly strategic. Apple uses its own wireless protocol for AirDrop, which is built into the iOS and macOS operating systems. Android uses Google's Nearby Connections API. These protocols are fundamentally incompatible.

From a business perspective, making cross-platform transfer easy would reduce one of the barriers that keeps people in a single ecosystem. If you can effortlessly send files between iPhone and Android, switching platforms becomes less daunting.

The Workarounds People Use

Before discovering P2P browser transfer, most people resort to one of these workarounds:

  • Email: Send files to yourself as email attachments. Limited to 25MB per attachment on most providers.
  • Cloud storage: Upload to Google Drive or Dropbox from the iPhone, then download on the Android. Requires accounts and takes twice as long.
  • Social media: Send files through WhatsApp, Telegram, or other messaging apps. Quality may be reduced for photos and videos.
  • Bluetooth: Theoretically possible but extremely slow and unreliable between different platforms.
  • USB with adapter: Requires a Lightning-to-USB-C adapter and the right cables. Not always available when you need them.

Every one of these methods has significant limitations. P2P browser transfer eliminates all of them.

Why AirDrop Does Not Work With Android

AirDrop is Apple's file sharing protocol, and it is excellent — when you are sharing between Apple devices. It is fast, reliable, and requires no setup. But it is completely locked to the Apple ecosystem.

How AirDrop Works

AirDrop uses a combination of Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) for device discovery and Apple Direct WiFi (AWDL) for the actual file transfer. Both of these are proprietary Apple technologies that Android devices do not support.

When you AirDrop a file from an iPhone to a Mac, the iPhone uses BLE to find nearby Apple devices, establishes an AWDL connection, and transfers the file at high speed. The entire process is automatic and seamless.

Why You Cannot AirDrop to Android

Android phones do not have AWDL support. Even if they could discover an iPhone via Bluetooth, they cannot establish the high-speed WiFi connection that AirDrop requires. This is not a limitation that can be fixed with an app — it is baked into the hardware and operating system.

Some third-party apps claim to enable AirDrop-like functionality between iPhone and Android, but they typically use standard Bluetooth or create a local WiFi hotspot, which is slow and unreliable. They are not true AirDrop alternatives.

The Right Solution: Use Standards, Not Proprietary Protocols

The solution to the AirDrop problem is to use a technology that both platforms support. Every modern iPhone and Android phone has a web browser. Every web browser supports WebSocket connections. This means you can create a direct connection between any two devices using nothing but their browsers.

This is exactly how 4share works. It does not rely on proprietary protocols, does not require specific hardware support, and works on any device with a modern web browser.

The P2P Browser Solution

Browser-based P2P transfer is the most practical solution for cross-platform file sharing. Here is why it works so well for iPhone-to-Android transfers.

Universal Compatibility

Every iPhone has Safari. Every Android phone has Chrome (or another browser). Both support the WebRTC standard, which enables direct peer-to-peer connections between devices. This means browser-based P2P transfer works on every iPhone and every Android phone, regardless of model, operating system version, or carrier.

No App Installation

App-based file transfer tools require both devices to install the same app. This means the Android user has to download an app from the Play Store, and the iPhone user has to download the same app from the App Store. With browser-based transfer, neither device needs to install anything.

No Account Creation

Most file transfer apps require you to create an account with an email address and password. Browser-based P2P transfer requires no account. You open a website, select your files, and share a code. That is it.

Direct Transfer

Browser-based P2P transfer creates a direct connection between the two devices. The file data goes straight from the iPhone to the Android phone without touching any server. This is faster than cloud-based transfer and more private.

Step-by-Step: iPhone to Android Transfer

Here is the complete process for transferring files from an iPhone to an Android phone using 4share.

On the iPhone (Sending Device)

  1. Open Safari on the iPhone.
  2. Navigate to 4share.work.
  3. Tap the file selection area and choose the files you want to send. You can select multiple files at once.
  4. 4share will display a connection code or QR code. This code is what the Android device needs to connect.

On the Android Phone (Receiving Device)

  1. Open Chrome (or any browser) on the Android phone.
  2. Navigate to 4share.work.
  3. Enter the connection code provided by the iPhone, or scan the QR code if the devices are nearby.
  4. The connection establishes and the files begin transferring immediately.

During the Transfer

Both devices will show a progress indicator. You can see the transfer speed, percentage complete, and estimated time remaining. Both devices must remain powered on and connected to WiFi throughout the transfer.

For small files (photos, documents), the transfer typically completes in seconds. For larger files (videos, archives), the transfer may take a few minutes depending on file size and network speed.

After the Transfer

Once the progress bar reaches 100%, the files are on the Android device. They will be available in the Android's download folder or in the location specified by the browser. You can disconnect both devices and use them normally.

Transferring Photos and Videos

Photos and videos are the most common files people transfer between iPhone and Android. Here is how to handle them specifically.

Transferring Photos

iPhone photos are stored in the Photos app as HEIC files (for newer iPhones) or JPEG files (for older models). Both formats are universally supported, so the Android device will be able to open them without any conversion.

To transfer photos:

  1. On the iPhone, you can either select individual photos in the Photos app and share them, or use 4share to select multiple photos from your camera roll.
  2. If you want to transfer a large number of photos, it is more efficient to select them all at once rather than sending them one by one.
  3. The Android device receives the photos and saves them to its gallery or downloads folder.

Transferring Videos

iPhone videos can be very large, especially 4K recordings. A one-minute 4K video from an iPhone can be 400MB or more. P2P transfer handles these large files well because it transfers at the full speed of your WiFi network.

For videos, it is important to keep both devices connected to the same WiFi network for maximum speed. A 1GB video file on a fast WiFi network will transfer in about 10 seconds.

Preserving Video Quality

Unlike messaging apps that compress videos, P2P transfer sends the original file without any quality loss. The video you receive on the Android device is identical to the one on the iPhone — same resolution, same bitrate, same quality.

Transferring Documents and Files

Documents, PDFs, spreadsheets, and other files transfer the same way as photos and videos. The process is identical.

Finding Files on iPhone

iPhone files are spread across several apps: the Files app, the Notes app, the Mail app (attachments), and third-party apps. To send a file via P2P transfer, you need to locate it first.

The easiest approach is to use the Files app, which serves as the central file manager on iPhone. If you have saved files to iCloud Drive or local storage, they will appear in the Files app and can be selected for transfer.

Sending Multiple Files at Once

4share supports multi-file selection, so you can select all the documents you want to transfer and send them in a single batch. This is much faster than sending them one at a time.

File Format Compatibility

Most document formats are cross-platform compatible. PDFs, plain text files, and images open on both iPhone and Android. Microsoft Office formats (DOCX, XLSX, PPTX) can be opened on Android using the Microsoft Office app or Google Docs. Apple-specific formats like Pages, Numbers, and Keynote may not open on Android without conversion.

Moving Contacts Between Platforms

Contacts require a slightly different approach because they are stored in a database format, not as regular files.

Exporting Contacts from iPhone

  1. Open the Contacts app on the iPhone.
  2. Go to Settings and select "Export vCard" or similar option.
  3. This creates a VCF file containing all your contacts.
  4. The VCF file can be transferred using P2P transfer just like any other file.

Importing Contacts on Android

  1. Receive the VCF file on the Android phone via P2P transfer.
  2. Open the Contacts app on the Android phone.
  3. Tap the menu and select "Import" or "Import from file."
  4. Select the VCF file you received.
  5. All contacts will be added to your Android contacts list.

Sending Large Files Cross-Platform

P2P browser transfer excels at large file transfer between iPhone and Android. Here are some scenarios and tips.

Large Video Files

4K videos, screen recordings, and long video clips can easily exceed several gigabytes. P2P transfer handles these files without any issues. The transfer speed is limited only by your WiFi network.

App Data and Backups

Some app data can be exported as files and transferred. For example, you can export chat histories, game saves, or app settings as files and send them to the Android device. However, not all app data is portable between platforms.

Music Libraries

Music files (MP3, AAC, FLAC) transfer perfectly between iPhone and Android. If you have music stored locally on your iPhone (not in Apple Music's DRM-protected format), you can transfer the files to your Android phone.

Tips for Faster Transfers

Here are practical tips to speed up your iPhone-to-Android file transfers.

Use the Same WiFi Network

Both devices must be connected to the same WiFi network for local transfer. This is the single most important factor in transfer speed.

Use 5GHz WiFi

If your router supports both 2.4GHz and 5GHz bands, connect both devices to the 5GHz band for faster speeds.

Keep Devices Close to the Router

WiFi signal strength directly affects transfer speed. Keep both devices in the same room as the router for the best results.

Close Background Apps

Apps running in the background consume bandwidth and processing power. Close unnecessary apps on both devices before starting the transfer.

Disable Low Power Mode on iPhone

Low Power Mode on iPhone reduces network performance to save battery. Disable it before starting a large transfer.

Privacy Considerations

Cross-platform file transfer raises important privacy questions.

Data Does Not Leave Your Network

With local P2P transfer, the file data travels directly between the two devices over your WiFi network. It does not go through the internet, so your internet service provider cannot see it.

No Third-Party Access

Unlike cloud-based transfer, no third party has access to your files during the transfer. The data is encrypted and goes directly from one device to the other.

No Account Data

P2P browser transfer requires no account, so there is no personal data stored on any server. You do not need to provide an email address, phone number, or any identifying information.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I transfer files from iPhone to Android without any app?

Yes. With browser-based P2P tools like 4share, you do not need to install any app on either device. Both the iPhone and the Android phone use their web browsers to establish a direct connection.

Do iPhone and Android use different file systems?

Yes. iPhones use APFS and Android phones use ext4 or f2fs. However, this does not affect file transfer. Files are transferred as-is, and the receiving device opens them using its native apps or compatible software.

What is the fastest way to transfer large files from iPhone to Android?

The fastest method is peer-to-peer transfer over your local WiFi network. Both devices connect to the same WiFi, and the file transfers directly between them at WiFi speed, bypassing the internet entirely.

Can I transfer photos from iPhone to Android wirelessly?

Yes. P2P file transfer works with all file types, including photos. Select the photos on your iPhone, share the connection code, and the Android device receives them directly.

How do I transfer contacts from iPhone to Android?

Contact transfer is a separate process from file transfer. Export your iPhone contacts as a VCF file, then send that file to your Android device using P2P transfer. The Android contacts app can import VCF files.

Is AirDrop compatible with Android?

No. AirDrop is an Apple-proprietary protocol that only works between Apple devices. Android devices cannot receive AirDrop transfers. You need a cross-platform solution like P2P browser-based transfer.

Can I send WhatsApp chats from iPhone to Android?

WhatsApp chats are encrypted and tied to the app. They cannot be sent as regular files. Use WhatsApp's built-in chat transfer feature when setting up WhatsApp on the new Android device.

What files can I transfer from iPhone to Android?

You can transfer any file stored on your iPhone, including photos, videos, documents, music, and app files. Some files may need compatible apps on the Android side to open.

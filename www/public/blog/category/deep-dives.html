Understanding the Technology

File transfer might seem simple on the surface — you select a file, share a code, and the file arrives on the other device. But beneath that simple user experience is a sophisticated stack of technologies that make it all work. WebRTC creates direct browser-to-browser connections. ICE navigates the complex landscape of routers and firewalls. DTLS encrypts the data. NAT traversal techniques punch holes through network barriers.

This category dives deep into these technologies. We explain how WebRTC works under the hood, how peer-to-peer networks are established and maintained, how NAT traversal solves the problem of connecting devices behind routers, and how different file transfer protocols compare. These articles are for readers who want to understand not just how to use file transfer tools, but how they actually work.

Whether you are a developer building file sharing applications, a student learning about networking, or simply a curious user who wants to understand the technology, these deep dives provide the technical depth you need.

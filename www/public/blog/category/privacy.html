Understanding how your files are protected during transfer. Encryption, server storage risks, anonymous sharing, and privacy best practices.

Why Privacy Matters in File Transfer

Every file you send contains information. A photo reveals where you were and what you were doing. A document might contain financial data, medical information, or business secrets. A video captures moments you intended to share with specific people, not with a service provider's servers.

When you use a cloud-based file sharing service, your files are uploaded to a server that is controlled by someone else. That someone can read your files (if they choose to). Their employees can access your files. Their servers can be breached by attackers. Their infrastructure can be subpoenaed by governments. Your files are out of your control the moment they leave your device.

Privacy in file transfer means maintaining control over who can access your files. It means your files are encrypted so that only the intended recipient can read them. It means your files are not stored on any third-party server where they could be accessed, analyzed, or exposed.

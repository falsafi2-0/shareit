To send files from your phone to a Smart TV, use a peer-to-peer browser-based transfer tool like 4share. Open the web browser on your Smart TV, open 4share.work on your phone, select the files, and enter the connection code on the TV. The files transfer directly over WiFi and are saved on the TV for playback. This works for photos, videos, music, and any other file type — no USB drives, no cables, no casting required.

Smart TVs have become the center of home entertainment. They stream movies, display photos, play music, and run apps. But one thing they do not do well is receive files from your phone. Most people resort to USB drives, casting, or complex DLNA setups to get media from their phone to the TV.

P2P browser transfer provides a much simpler solution. If your Smart TV has a web browser — and most modern ones do — you can transfer files directly from your phone to the TV in seconds.

Why Send Files to Your Smart TV?

There are several compelling reasons to transfer files directly to your Smart TV.

Family Photo Sharing

Displaying family photos on a large TV screen is a great way to share memories with family and guests. Instead of crowding around a phone screen, everyone can enjoy photos on the big screen. Transferring photos to the TV lets you display them without keeping your phone occupied.

Playing Personal Videos

Videos from your phone — vacation clips, birthday parties, children's milestones — look incredible on a large TV screen. Transferring these videos to the TV lets you play them without the phone's small screen and limited speakers.

Music Playback

If your Smart TV has good speakers, playing your personal music library through the TV can fill the room with sound. Transferring music files to the TV lets you play them without keeping your phone connected.

Presentations and Displays

In a home office or meeting room, transferring presentation files to the TV lets you display them on a large screen for everyone to see.

Permanent Availability

Once files are transferred to the TV, they are available anytime — even when your phone is not in the room. This is more convenient than casting, which requires the phone to be actively connected.

Traditional Methods and Their Limitations

Here is why common methods for getting files to your TV fall short.

USB Drive

USB drive transfer is reliable but inconvenient. You need a USB drive, you need to copy files from your phone to the drive (which may require an adapter), and then copy them from the drive to the TV. For a quick photo display, this process is too cumbersome.

Casting (Chromecast, AirPlay)

Casting streams content from your phone to the TV in real time. It works well for streaming but has limitations:

  • The phone must be on and connected throughout the playback.
  • The phone cannot be used for other purposes during casting.
  • Casting uses the phone's battery.
  • The quality may be reduced depending on your WiFi connection.
  • Not all apps support casting.

DLNA / Media Servers

DLNA allows your TV to access media files on your phone over the network. However, DLNA setup is complex, requires compatible apps on both devices, and is unreliable on many networks.

Email to Yourself

Some people email files to themselves and then access them on the TV through a browser. This works for small files but is impractical for large videos or batches of photos.

Using P2P Transfer to Send Files to Your TV

P2P browser transfer is the simplest method for getting files from your phone to your Smart TV.

How It Works

Smart TVs with web browsers can access 4share.work just like any other website. The TV's browser connects to the phone's browser, and the file transfers directly over WiFi.

What You Need

  • A Smart TV with a web browser (Samsung, LG, Sony, TCL, and most other modern Smart TVs have built-in browsers).
  • A phone with a web browser (any modern smartphone).
  • Both devices connected to the same WiFi network.

Advantages Over Other Methods

  • No USB drive needed: Transfer happens wirelessly.
  • No casting required: The file is saved on the TV, not streamed from the phone.
  • No phone required during playback: Once transferred, the TV plays the file independently.
  • No app installation: Just use the TV's built-in browser.
  • No account required: 4share works without any account creation.

Setting Up the Browser on Your Smart TV

Before transferring files, you need to access the web browser on your Smart TV.

Samsung Smart TV

Press the Home button on your remote, navigate to the Apps section, and find the "Internet" or "Web Browser" app. Open it and navigate to 4share.work.

LG Smart TV

Press the Home button on your remote, navigate to the Web Browser app (usually in the ribbon at the bottom). Open it and navigate to 4share.work.

Sony Android TV

Press the Home button, navigate to Apps, and find the "Internet" app. If no browser is installed, you can install one from the Google Play Store on your TV.

TCL Roku TV

Roku TVs do not have a built-in web browser. You can use the Roku mobile app to cast, or connect a streaming device (like a Fire TV Stick) that has a browser.

Navigating with a TV Remote

Using a web browser with a TV remote is slower than using a computer. Use the directional pad to navigate, the OK button to select, and the back button to go back. Some TVs support USB keyboards or Bluetooth keyboards, which make browser navigation much faster.

Sending Photos to Your Smart TV

Here is the step-by-step process for transferring photos from your phone to your Smart TV.

Step 1: Open the Browser on the TV

Open the web browser on your Smart TV and navigate to 4share.work.

Step 2: Open 4share on the Phone

On your phone, open the browser and navigate to 4share.work.

Step 3: Select Photos on the Phone

On the phone, tap the file selection area and select the photos you want to transfer. You can select multiple photos at once.

Step 4: Get the Connection Code

4share generates a connection code on the phone. This code is displayed on the phone screen.

Step 5: Enter the Code on the TV

Using the TV remote, navigate to the connection code field on 4share.work and enter the code from the phone. This may be slow with a TV remote — consider using a USB keyboard for faster input.

Step 6: Photos Transfer

The photos transfer from the phone to the TV over WiFi. Monitor the progress on both screens.

Step 7: View Photos on the TV

Once the transfer completes, the photos are saved on the TV. Open the TV's photo viewer or file manager to view them.

Step 8: Create a Slideshow

Most Smart TVs have a photo viewer that can display a slideshow of all photos in a folder. Select the transferred photos and start the slideshow for a family photo viewing experience.

Sending Videos to Your Smart TV

Videos are the most popular files to transfer to Smart TVs.

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Open the browser on the TV and navigate to 4share.work.
  2. Open 4share on the phone and select the video file.
  3. Get the connection code from the phone.
  4. Enter the code on the TV using the remote (or a connected keyboard).
  5. The video transfers over WiFi. Monitor the progress.
  6. Once complete, open the TV's media player and play the video.

Video Playback Tips

  • Format compatibility: MP4 is the most universally supported format on Smart TVs. If your video is in MOV or MKV format, check that your TV supports it.
  • Resolution: Most modern Smart TVs support 4K playback. Make sure your video resolution matches or is lower than your TV's resolution.
  • Audio: If the video has surround sound, make sure your TV or sound system supports it.

Large Video Files

For large video files (multi-gigabyte 4K recordings), the transfer may take several minutes. Keep both the phone and TV connected to WiFi throughout the transfer. For best results, connect both devices to the 5GHz WiFi band.

Casting vs. File Transfer

Understanding the difference between casting and file transfer helps you choose the right method.

Casting

Casting streams content from your phone to the TV in real time. The phone sends video and audio data to the TV, which displays it. The file is not saved on the TV — it is streamed from the phone.

  • Pros: No transfer time, works with streaming apps (Netflix, YouTube), no storage needed on TV.
  • Cons: Phone must be on and connected, phone cannot be used for other tasks, battery drain, quality may be reduced.

File Transfer

File transfer copies the file from the phone to the TV. The file is saved on the TV and can be played independently of the phone.

  • Pros: Phone can be used for other tasks, no battery drain, consistent quality, file available anytime.
  • Cons: Requires storage space on TV, transfer time for large files.

When to Use Each

  • Use casting for streaming content from apps (Netflix, YouTube, Spotify) and for quick, temporary viewing.
  • Use file transfer for personal media (photos, videos, music) that you want to keep on the TV for repeated viewing.

  • Audio: MP3, AAC, WAV, FLAC, OGG, WMA
  • Photos: JPEG, PNG, BMP, GIF, WEBP

Checking Your TV's Supported Formats

Check your TV's user manual or specifications page for the complete list of supported formats. Most manufacturers list supported formats on their website.

Tips for TV File Transfer

Here are practical tips for the best TV file transfer experience.

Use a USB Keyboard

Navigating a web browser with a TV remote is slow. Connect a USB keyboard to your TV for much faster navigation and text input. This makes entering connection codes much easier.

Use the 5GHz WiFi Band

Connect both your phone and TV to the 5GHz WiFi band for faster transfers. The 5GHz band is typically less congested and offers higher speeds.

Transfer During Setup

If you know you will want to display photos or play videos on the TV, transfer the files before the event. This avoids making guests wait while files transfer.

Organize Files in Folders

Create folders on your TV for different types of media — Photos, Videos, Music. This makes it easier to find and play transferred files.

Check Available Storage

Smart TVs have limited internal storage. Check how much storage is available before transferring large files. Some TVs support external USB storage for additional space.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I send files from my phone to my Smart TV?

Yes. If your Smart TV has a web browser, you can use P2P browser transfer to send files directly from your phone to the TV. Open 4share.work on both devices and transfer the files.

What if my Smart TV does not have a web browser?

Most modern Smart TVs (Samsung, LG, Sony, TCL) have built-in web browsers. If yours does not, you can use a streaming device (Roku, Fire TV, Apple TV) that has a browser, or transfer to a computer connected to the TV.

Can I play phone videos on my Smart TV?

Yes. Once you transfer a video file to your Smart TV, you can play it using the TV's media player app. MP4 and MKV formats are universally supported on modern Smart TVs.

How do I share photos from my phone to the TV?

Use P2P browser transfer to send photos from your phone to the Smart TV. The photos are saved on the TV and can be viewed using the TV's photo viewer or media player.

Do I need a USB drive to transfer files to my TV?

No. P2P browser transfer works wirelessly over WiFi. No USB drive, no cables, no physical media required.

Can I cast from my phone to the TV instead?

Casting (Chromecast, AirPlay, Miracast) streams the video in real time but does not save the file on the TV. P2P transfer saves the file on the TV for later viewing without the phone.

What video formats can Smart TVs play?

Most Smart TVs support MP4, MKV, AVI, and MOV formats. Some TVs also support WMV, FLV, and WebM. Check your TV's specifications for the full list of supported formats.

Can I transfer files from iPhone to Android TV?

Yes. Android TV has a built-in browser or can install one from the Play Store. Use P2P browser transfer to send files from any phone to an Android TV.

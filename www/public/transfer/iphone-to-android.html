Why iPhone to Android File Transfer Is So Hard

Apple and Google have built their ecosystems to keep you inside their walls. AirDrop works beautifully between Apple devices. Nearby Share (now Quick Share) works between Android devices. But when you need to move a file from an iPhone to an Android phone, you are on your own.

The traditional options are frustrating. Email has file size limits. Cloud storage requires accounts on both ends and eats into your free tier. Messaging apps like WhatsApp compress your photos and videos. Bluetooth is painfully slow and often fails to connect across platforms. You end up with a workflow that should take seconds dragging on for minutes or even longer.

This problem affects millions of people every day. Couples where one person has an iPhone and the other has an Android. Families with mixed devices. Coworkers who chose different phones. Students in group projects. The iPhone-to-Android transfer problem is universal, and most solutions are either inconvenient, slow, or require both parties to install the same app.

How 4share Solves the Problem

4share is a peer-to-peer file transfer service that works entirely in the web browser. It does not matter whether the sender has an iPhone and the receiver has an Android, or vice versa. Both devices open 4share.work in their browser, and the files transfer directly between them.

There is no app to install on either device. No account to create. No software compatibility to worry about. The service uses WebRTC, a technology that all modern browsers support, to create a direct connection between the two devices. Your files travel directly from one phone to the other without touching any server.

This means the transfer is private, fast, and free. The files are not uploaded to any cloud service. They are not compressed. They are not stored anywhere. They simply move from one device to the other.

Step-by-Step: How to Transfer Files From iPhone to Android

On the iPhone (Sender)

Open Safari or Chrome on your iPhone and go to 4share.work. Tap the file selection area and choose the files you want to send. You can select photos from your camera roll, documents from your files app, or any other file stored on your phone. Once you have selected your files, tap the send button. 4share will generate a 6-digit code. Keep this screen open.

On the Android Phone (Receiver)

Open Chrome or any modern browser on the Android phone and go to 4share.work. You will see a field to enter a code. Type in the 6-digit code that the iPhone user shared with you. You can share this code via text message, phone call, or any other method. Once you enter the code, the transfer starts automatically.

Watch the Transfer

Both phones will show a progress indicator. The files transfer directly from the iPhone to the Android phone using your internet connection. When the transfer is complete, the files are saved on the Android phone. The original files remain on the iPhone.

Technical Details: How the Transfer Works

Under the hood, 4share uses WebRTC (Web Real-Time Communication) to create a direct peer-to-peer connection between the two devices. WebRTC is a standardized technology supported by all modern browsers, including Safari on iPhone and Chrome on Android.

When the sender selects files, 4share creates a temporary offer that encodes the file information. When the receiver enters the 6-digit code, 4share matches the two devices and establishes a direct connection. The files then stream directly from the sender's device to the receiver's device.

The connection is encrypted using DTLS (Datagram Transport Layer Security), which is the same encryption technology used in VPN connections. This means no one can intercept or read your files during transfer, even if they are on the same network.

The 6-digit code serves as a temporary access key. It expires after a short time and can only be used once for each transfer. This prevents unauthorized access to your files.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to install an app on my iPhone?

No. 4share works entirely in Safari or Chrome on your iPhone. There is no app to install. Just open 4share.work in your browser.

Do I need to install an app on the Android phone?

No. 4share works in any modern browser on Android, including Chrome, Firefox, and Samsung Internet. No app installation required.

Do both people need a 4share account?

No. Neither person needs an account. 4share is completely account-free. You just open the website and start transferring.

Will my photos lose quality during transfer?

No. 4share transfers files in their original format and quality. There is no compression applied to any file. What you send is exactly what the receiver gets.

Is there a limit on how many files I can send?

No. You can send as many files as you want in a single transfer. There is no limit on the number of files or their total size.

Does it work over cellular data?

Yes. 4share works over any internet connection — WiFi, cellular data, or Ethernet. The transfer speed depends on the connection speed of both devices.

What if I close the browser during a transfer?

If you close the browser, the transfer will be interrupted. However, if both devices keep their browsers open, the transfer will continue until it completes. For very large files, make sure to keep the browser open on both devices.

Can I send files from Android to iPhone too?

Yes. 4share works in both directions. You can send from iPhone to Android, Android to iPhone, or between any two devices with a modern browser.
